Frequently Ask Questions

An online advertisement is a digital marketing message displayed on a website, app, or search engine results page to promote a product, service, or brand, aiming to reach potential customers.


Display ads: Banners, pop-ups, and graphical ads appearing on websites.
Search engine marketing (SEM): Ads displayed on search engine results pages based on keywords.
Social media ads: Sponsored posts on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter.
Native advertising: Content that blends seamlessly with the platform's design.
Video ads: Commercials played on video platforms like YouTube


A CTA is a clear instruction within an ad that tells the viewer what to do next, like "Shop Now," "Learn More," or "Sign Up."


Use demographics (age, gender, location), interests, behaviors, and custom audience lists based on your customer data to refine targeting on ad platforms.


Keywords are words or phrases relevant to your product or service that users might search for, allowing you to display ads when those keywords are entered on search engines.


Google Ads is a platform where businesses can create text ads to appear on Google search results pages, based on keywords users search for.
